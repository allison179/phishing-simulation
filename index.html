🚩 Red Flags in That Email

Here is exactly why that HR reward offer should have raised suspicion immediately:

  • ⚠️
    Fake Sender Domain The email came from corphr@gmail.com — a free public Gmail account. Rossari Biotech's HR department exclusively uses @rossari.com addresses. Any HR communication from a Gmail, Yahoo, or other free email provider is fraudulent.
  • ⚠️
    Urgency & Pressure Tactics The message created artificial urgency ("Claim your reward within 24 hours!"). This is a classic manipulation technique designed to prevent you from thinking critically and verifying the email's authenticity before acting.
  • ⚠️
    Request to Click a Link & Enter Credentials Legitimate Rossari Biotech HR systems will never ask you to verify your identity by clicking an unsolicited email link. Any email directing you to log in via an unexpected link — especially from a non-company address — is a phishing attack.

⚡ Common Risks You Might Encounter

Understanding these threats is the first step to defending against them:

🎣
High Risk
Phishing

Attackers send fake emails to steal your passwords or financial information. You must be extra cautious with any unexpected email asking for sensitive data or directing you to click a link.

🦠
High Risk
Malware & Ransomware

Malicious programs that can corrupt or steal your data and crash company systems. They enter your computer through unsafe links, email attachments, or unauthorised software downloads.

🤦
Medium Risk
Human Error

Accidental mistakes — such as opening an infected email or deleting critical data — can lead to data breaches that disrupt company operations and harm Rossari Biotech's reputation.

🔓
Medium Risk
Weak Access Controls

Weak or shared passwords can expose sensitive company information, leading to financial loss and reputational damage. Use strong, unique passwords and enable MFA on all accounts.

🛡️ Your Role in Staying Safe

Every employee at Rossari Biotech is a critical part of our cyber defence. Here is what is expected of you:

  • 👁️
    Stay Vigilant You are expected to remain alert at all times and look for signs of suspicious activity in emails, messages, and any digital communication you receive.
  • 🔍
    Verify Before You Click Before clicking any link in an email, always verify that it is authentic. Hover over links to check the real destination URL and confirm the sender's full email address matches an official Rossari Biotech domain.
  • 📣
    Report Suspicious Activity If you notice anything unusual, you must report it to the IT department immediately — do not wait. Early reporting can stop an attack from spreading across the entire organisation.
  • 📋
    Follow IT Policies You must strictly adhere to all IT security policies, including changing your passwords at the intervals required by company applications and not sharing credentials with colleagues.
  • 🎓
    Participate in Training You are required to attend regular IT security awareness training sessions to stay updated on emerging threats and how to handle them effectively.

💡 Key Takeaways

  • 🔗
    Hover before you click. Always hover over links to preview the destination URL. If the domain looks unfamiliar, misspelled, or uses a URL shortener — do not click it.
  • 📧
    Verify the sender's full email address. Display names are trivially spoofed. Always check the actual address — Rossari Biotech communications come only from @rossari.com addresses.
  • 🔑
    Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Even if your password is compromised, MFA provides a critical second layer of defence. Enable it on all business applications immediately.
  • 📣
    Report — don't just delete. Reporting helps IT block the sender for everyone. Use the "Report Phishing" button in your email client, or forward suspicious emails to it-helpdesk@rossari.com.
  • ⏸️
    Pause under pressure. Any email that demands urgency ("Act now or lose access!") is trying to bypass your critical thinking. That feeling of urgency is itself a red flag — slow down and verify before acting.
